Have you ever heard of the Manhattan Project? Now what about the Hydrogen bomb? These 2 are very closely related in that both were using applications of 2 very related chemical phenomenon. That is, nuclear fusion and nuclear fission.

The Big Bombs of Today, and From Now On

The Manhattan Project focused on bombs from nuclear fission. Simply put the explosive force generated from the energy a reaction where the nucleus of an atom splits. The Hydrogen Bomb, otherwise known as a thermonuclear bomb, focused on nuclear fusion. Essentially the opposite of fission; where 2 lighter atomic nuclei combine to form a heaver one, releasing massive amounts of energy.

The military bombs of today are a result of these 2 experiments, research, and lasting effects. Modern explosives are continuing to expand in variety, form, shape, and dangerousness.
